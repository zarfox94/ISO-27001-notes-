# ISO Standards and Accreditation Framework

## Overview

ISO 27001 certification operates within a broader **international accreditation framework**.

This framework ensures that certifications are:

* credible
* consistent
* internationally recognized

Several standards and organizations govern how certification bodies conduct audits.

These include:

* ISO/IEC 17021
* ISO/IEC 27006
* International Accreditation Forum (IAF)
* IAF Mandatory Documents (MD)

Together, these components ensure that ISO 27001 certifications are **reliable and globally trusted**.

---

# Certification Bodies vs Accreditation Bodies

Understanding ISO certification requires distinguishing between two types of organizations.

## Certification Bodies (CB)

Certification Bodies are organizations that **perform ISO audits and issue certificates**.

Examples include:

* BSI
* TÜV
* SGS
* Bureau Veritas
* LRQA

Certification bodies conduct:

* Stage 1 audits
* Stage 2 audits
* Surveillance audits
* Recertification audits

However, certification bodies themselves must be **accredited**.

---

## Accreditation Bodies (AB)

Accreditation Bodies supervise certification bodies.

They verify that certification bodies perform audits **competently and impartially**.

Examples include:

| Country        | Accreditation Body |
| -------------- | ------------------ |
| United Kingdom | UKAS               |
| United States  | ANAB               |
| Germany        | DAkkS              |
| France         | COFRAC             |
| Ireland        | INAB               |

Accreditation bodies ensure that certification bodies follow international rules when issuing ISO certifications.

---

# International Accreditation Forum (IAF)

The **International Accreditation Forum (IAF)** is a global organization that oversees accreditation systems.

Its purpose is to ensure that ISO certifications are **recognized across different countries**.

IAF manages agreements such as the:

### IAF Multilateral Recognition Arrangement (MLA)

The MLA ensures that certifications issued in one country are accepted in others.

For example:

If a company is certified by an accredited body in Germany, that certification will be recognized in the United States or Japan.

This reduces the need for **duplicate certifications across markets**.

---

# ISO/IEC 17021

**ISO/IEC 17021** defines the general requirements for certification bodies that audit management systems.

This standard ensures that certification bodies operate with:

* competence
* impartiality
* consistency

Key aspects include:

### Auditor Competence

Auditors must demonstrate:

* technical knowledge
* auditing skills
* industry understanding

Certification bodies must maintain evidence of auditor competence.

---

### Impartiality

Certification bodies must avoid conflicts of interest.

Examples:

* auditors cannot audit organizations they have consulted
* auditors must remain independent

This ensures audit credibility.

---

### Audit Process Standardization

ISO 17021 defines requirements for:

* audit planning
* evidence collection
* audit reporting
* certification decisions

This ensures that audits follow a **consistent methodology worldwide**.

---

# ISO/IEC 27006

ISO/IEC 27006 builds on ISO 17021 and defines **additional requirements for ISO 27001 audits**.

While ISO 17021 applies to all management system certifications, ISO 27006 specifically addresses **information security management systems**.

Key areas covered include:

### Auditor Expertise

Auditors must demonstrate knowledge of:

* information security principles
* risk management
* security controls
* information technology environments

This ensures that auditors are capable of evaluating complex security systems.

---

### Audit Methodology

ISO 27006 specifies how certification bodies must conduct ISMS audits.

This includes:

* audit duration
* sampling approaches
* technical evaluation methods

These requirements ensure that ISO 27001 audits are **thorough and technically sound**.

---

# IAF Mandatory Documents (MD)

The IAF publishes **Mandatory Documents (MD)** that certification bodies must follow.

These documents provide additional guidance on how certification processes should operate.

---

## IAF MD 1 — Multi-Site Audits

Defines how audits should be conducted for organizations with multiple locations.

It explains:

* site sampling strategies
* classification of permanent, temporary, and virtual sites

This helps auditors evaluate large organizations efficiently.

---

## IAF MD 4 — Use of ICT in Auditing

Defines rules for **remote audits** using technology.

Examples include:

* video conferencing
* remote system access
* digital documentation review

Remote audits became particularly important during the COVID-19 pandemic.

---

## IAF MD 26 — Transition Requirements

Defines how organizations transition between **versions of ISO standards**.

Example:

When ISO 27001 was revised in 2022, organizations previously certified under ISO 27001:2013 had to transition to the new version within a defined timeframe.

Typical transition periods range from **1 to 3 years**.
